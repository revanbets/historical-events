          HISTODB MOBILE APP — USER GUIDE
          For iPhone & Android
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Welcome to HistoDB — the mobile companion to the Historical Events Research
Database. This guide explains how to use every part of the app in plain
language. No technical knowledge needed.

PART 1 — GETTING STARTED
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1: Get the app on your phone
──────────────────────────────────
  A) Install "Expo Go" from the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play (Android).
     It's free and published by "Expo Project."

  B) Your administrator will share a QR code or a link that looks like:
       exp.host/... or a standard QR code
     Open Expo Go → tap "Scan QR Code" → scan it.
     The HistoDB app loads on your phone.

  Note: You need internet access for this. The app connects to the cloud
  database — it doesn't store all events locally on your device.


STEP 2: Log in
──────────────
  When the app opens you'll see the Login screen.

  • Enter your username and password (same as the desktop website).
  • Tap "Sign In."

  DEFAULT ACCOUNTS (for initial testing):
    Username: owner     Password: owner123   (full access)
    Username: admin     Password: admin123   (admin)
    Username: testuser  Password: test123    (basic user)

  After logging in once, the app remembers your session so you don't have to
  log in every single time.

  FACE ID / TOUCH ID (iPhone/Android biometrics):
  After you've logged in with your password once, a "Use Face ID" (or
  "Use Fingerprint") button appears on the login screen. Tap it to log in
  instantly with your face or fingerprint next time — no typing needed.

  📋  EVENTS     — Browse and search all events in the database
  🔬  RESEARCH   — Research session hub (the main mobile-exclusive feature)

The Home tab shows you a snapshot of everything:

  • Total Events — how many events are in the database
  • Total Sources — total uploaded source documents
  • Active Research Session — if you have a session open, a banner appears

    Tap it to go straight to that session.
  • Recent Events — the most recently added events. Tap any event card to
    open its full detail page.
  • Quick Actions — buttons to jump to Research or Add screens.

PART 4 — EVENTS TAB (Browse the Database)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This is where you search and browse every event in the database.

SEARCHING:
  • The search bar at the top searches titles and descriptions.
  • Just start typing — results update as you type.
  • Tap the X in the search bar to clear it.

FILTERING:
  • Tap the "Filters" button to open the filter panel.
  • Filter by: date range, topics, people, organizations, research level,
    source type, and more.
  • Tap "Apply" to see filtered results. Tap "Clear" to remove all filters.

SORTING:
  • Tap "Sort" to change the order: newest first, oldest first, A-Z, etc.

READING AN EVENT:
  • Tap any event card to open the full Event Detail page.
  • The detail page shows: full description, key topics, people mentioned,
    organizations involved, source links, AI summary, and related events.
  • Tap a topic, person, or organization chip to see all events connected
    to that entity.
  • Tap a source link to open it in your phone's browser.

PART 5 — RESEARCH TAB (The Big Mobile Feature)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Research tab is the mobile app's most powerful screen. It's designed for
when you're scrolling through TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, X, or any other
app and find something worth saving for research.

On desktop, you'd use the Chrome browser extension. On mobile, you have two
ways to capture content — both are described below.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW RESEARCH SESSIONS WORK (overview)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A "Research Session" is a container for a batch of related sources you're
collecting. Think of it like a folder:

  1. You start a session (give it a name like "FEMA investigation")
  2. You browse other apps and capture URLs into it
  3. When done browsing, you come back and tap "Analyze & Save"
  4. The AI reads all your captured links and saves them as events to the
     database — all in one go.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METHOD 1 — CLIPBOARD DETECTION (works automatically, no setup)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This is the easiest method. It works with every app automatically.

HOW IT WORKS:

  1. Open any other app — TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, X, Reddit, anything.
  2. Find a video or post you want to capture.
  3. Tap the "Share" button → tap "Copy Link" (exact words vary by app):
       • TikTok: tap "..." → "Copy Link"
       • Instagram: tap "..." → "Copy Link"
       • YouTube: tap "Share" → "Copy Link"
       • X (Twitter): tap "Share" → "Copy post link"
       • Any website: copy the URL from the address bar
  4. Switch back to HistoDB (tap its icon in your app switcher or home screen).
  5. As soon as HistoDB comes to the front, it checks your clipboard and
     shows a banner at the top:

       ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
       │  🎵 TikTok link detected                     │
       │  tiktok.com/@username/video/12345            │
       │  [Add to Session]  [Quick Analyze]  [Dismiss]│
       └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  6. Choose what to do:
       • "Add to Session" — saves the URL to your active research session
         without analyzing it yet. Best when you're collecting many links.
       • "Quick Analyze" — immediately sends it to the AI for analysis and
         creates an event. Best for one-off captures.
       • "Dismiss" — ignores this clipboard URL (it won't re-appear for
         this same link).

SUPPORTED PLATFORMS (auto-detected with logo and color):
  • TikTok      • Instagram     • YouTube       • X (Twitter)
  • Reddit       • Facebook      • Substack      • Any other website

You don't have to do anything special — just copy a link in any app and
switch back to HistoDB. The detection is automatic.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METHOD 2 — iOS SHORTCUT (one-tap capture without switching apps)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This takes 5 minutes to set up once, then works forever. It lets you tap
the standard "Share" button in any app and instantly capture to HistoDB —
without having to copy a link manually.

SETUP (one time only):

  1. Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone (it's built in — white icon with
     colored geometric shapes, usually in the Utilities folder).

  2. Tap the "+" button in the top-right corner.

  3. Tap "Add Action."

  4. In the search bar, type "URL" and tap "URL" (just the URL action).

  5. Tap the blue "+" circle below that first action to add another one.

  6. Search for "Open URLs" and tap it.

  7. In the "Open URLs" action, tap the "URL" field and type exactly:
       histodb://share?url=[Shortcut Input]

     To insert [Shortcut Input]:
       • Tap the URL field
       • Tap the "Variable" option (looks like a magic wand or "Insert Variable")
       • Choose "Shortcut Input"
       The field should now show: histodb://share?url=[Shortcut Input]

  8. Tap the settings icon (three dots or the name at the top) and:
       • Name it: "Add to HistoDB"
       • Tap "Add to Share Sheet" toggle to turn it ON

  9. Tap "Done."

HOW TO USE IT (after setup):

  1. In TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, X, Safari, or anywhere — tap the Share
     button (the box with an arrow pointing up).
  2. Scroll through the share options and tap "Add to HistoDB."
  3. HistoDB opens automatically with the URL ready to add to your session.

This is faster than Method 1 because you never have to copy and switch apps.


───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
STARTING & MANAGING SESSIONS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STARTING A SESSION:
  1. Go to the Research tab.
  2. Tap "Start New Session."
  3. Type a name (e.g., "CIA document dump" or "climate whistleblowers").
  4. Optionally add a description.
  5. Tap "Start Session."

WHILE A SESSION IS ACTIVE:
  • You can continue browsing and capturing URLs. Each one gets added to
    the session automatically when you tap "Add to Session."
  • You can also manually type or paste a URL in the "Quick Capture" box
    and tap "Add."
  • The session shows a running count of captures.

PAUSING A SESSION:
  • Tap "Pause" — the session stays saved. You can continue it tomorrow.
  • A paused session appears on the Research screen under "Your Sessions."
  • Tap "Resume" to continue where you left off.

ENDING & ANALYZING A SESSION:
  1. Tap the session name to open Session Detail.
  2. Tap "Analyze & Save All."
  3. The app sends each captured URL to the AI for analysis.
  4. A progress bar shows how many have been processed.
  5. When complete, all captures become full events in the database.
  6. Tap any result to open the new event.

VIEWING PAST SESSIONS:
  • The Research tab shows all your sessions (active, paused, and completed).
  • Tap any session to view its full trail and captures.

PART 6 — ADD TAB (Upload Sources)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Use this tab to upload a new source directly — without starting a full
research session. Good for single documents or quick uploads.

UPLOAD BY URL:
  • Paste any link: YouTube video, web article, tweet, TikTok, news site, etc.
  • Tap "Analyze URL."
  • The backend downloads the content, the AI analyzes it, and a new event
    is created with a summary, topics, people, and organizations automatically
    extracted.
  • For YouTube/videos: you can optionally set a start and end timestamp
    to analyze only a portion of the video (e.g., minutes 12:00 to 24:30).

CAMERA SCAN (iPhone/Android exclusive):
  • Tap "Scan Document" → the camera opens.
  • Point at a physical document (a printed article, a book page, a flyer).
  • Tap the shutter button.
  • The app sends the photo to the backend, which extracts the text using
    OCR (optical character recognition), then the AI analyzes it.

UPLOAD PHOTO / FILE FROM YOUR PHONE:
  • Tap "Choose from Photos" to pick an image from your camera roll.
  • Tap "Choose File" to pick a PDF, Word document, or text file from your
    phone's Files app.
  • The backend extracts the text and the AI creates a structured event.

AUTO-ANALYZE TOGGLE:
  • When ON:  AI analysis starts automatically as soon as you upload.
  • When OFF: You'll see a prompt after uploading asking whether to analyze.
              Good for when you're uploading multiple things and want to
              analyze them in batches to save cost.

MY UPLOADS:

PART 8 — FEATURES NOT ON MOBILE (and why)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These features are on the desktop website but not the mobile app:

  Network/Relationship Graph
    The graph that shows connections between events, people, and organizations
    uses a library that only works in a desktop web browser. A mobile version
    may be added in a future update.

  Presentations Editor
    Building slide presentations with drag-and-drop is very complex on a
    touch screen and is desktop-only for now. You can still view presentations
    on mobile.

  Spreadsheet View
    The wide data table is hard to use on a small screen. Mobile shows events
    in card format instead.

  Full Admin Panel
    Advanced admin controls (managing users, bulk edits) are desktop-only.

  All data is shared. Anything you add on mobile instantly appears on desktop
  and vice versa. You're not missing any data — just some specialized views.

PART 9 — TIPS & TRICKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

• RESEARCH WORKFLOW TIP: Before a heavy research session (e.g., going down a
  rabbit hole on TikTok or YouTube), start a session first. Then just copy
  every interesting link you see. Come back to HistoDB when done and tap
  "Analyze & Save All" — the AI handles all the descriptions and tags.

• AI ANALYSIS IS SLOW THE FIRST TIME: The AI server goes to sleep after
  15 minutes of no use. The very first analysis of the day takes ~30 seconds
  to wake it up. This is normal. Don't tap Analyze again — just wait.

• SAVE BATTERY: The app checks your clipboard only when you bring it to the
  front (it doesn't run in the background). There's no battery drain from
  keeping HistoDB installed.

• CLIPBOARD IS PRIVACY-RESPECTING: The app only reads your clipboard when you
  manually switch to it. It never reads clipboard data in the background.

• iOS SHORTCUT TIP: After you set up the Shortcut (Part 5, Method 2), add it
  to your Home Screen for one-tap access:
  Open Shortcuts app → long-press "Add to HistoDB" → "Add to Home Screen."

• SEARCHING EVENTS: On the Events tab, you can combine a text search with
  filters at the same time. For example: search "CIA" AND filter by
  "Research Level: High" to see only the most heavily sourced CIA events.

PART 10 — TROUBLESHOOTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  PROBLEM → WHAT TO TRY

  "Events not loading" / blank screens
    → Check your internet connection.
    → The database (Supabase) might have paused. Ask the admin to restore it.

  AI analysis says "failed" or nothing happens
    → Wait 30 seconds and try again — the server may be waking up.
    → If it fails 3 times in a row, the server may be down. Try again later.

  "Clipboard URL detected" banner isn't appearing
    → Make sure you copied the link BEFORE switching to HistoDB.
    → Some apps copy the URL differently — try "Share → Copy Link" instead
      of just copying text.
    → If it still doesn't work, paste the URL manually in the Research tab's
      Quick Capture box.

  App opens but shows a login error
    → Double-check your username and password (case-sensitive).
    → Ask the admin if your account is active.

  Face ID / Touch ID says "no saved session"
    → Log in once with your username and password first.
    → After that, biometric login works automatically.

  "SDK version incompatible" error (tech error)
    → Update the Expo Go app to the latest version in the App Store.
    → If the error continues, contact your administrator.

  App is very slow or freezes
    → Close and reopen the app.
    → If Expo Go is being used: close Expo Go completely, reopen, re-scan QR.
